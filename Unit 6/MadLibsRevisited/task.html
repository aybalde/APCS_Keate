Mad Libs Revisited

Recall the information from the MadLibs Lab. In 1953, a writer for the television show, The Honeymooners stumbled upon the idea of Mad Libs! Mad Libs is a game that asks those playing to provide different words based on their parts of speech without looking at a provided story. Once the words are put in the provided blanks, the story is read allowed and takes on a new, and often comical meaning because the provided words were given without context! You can read more about Mad Libs and it's creator in his New York Times obituary.

Assignment:
In the MadLibs Lab, the user only provided you with one of the parts of speech to fill in for all values of that part of speech. For example, you only took in one adjective, but you filled in that adjective 4 times because it appeared 4 times. This time, you will be asking the user for different values for each part of speech. For example, if your story has 4 adjectives, you will ask the user for 4 adjectives and use each of them once.

Use the provided Mad Libs story or one found here as your template. Each story gives different options of words one must provide. Write a program that asks a user to input different parts of speech as they are written and then print out the story with the words provided by the user. The program should be able to run repeatedly so users can enter a new list of words however many times they want to see different versions of the story with new words. Mad Libs provides parts of speech to the user, so you will as well. To review parts of speech, you can visit this site.
